Presentation Structure Guide

Phone Analysis Sales Deck - "Diagnose to Prescribe" Framework

The Medical Framework

This presentation follows a physician-friendly "Diagnose → Prescribe" narrative that mirrors clinical thinking:

Hook Build Trust
→
Diagnosis Show Symptoms
→
Prognosis Cost of Inaction
→
Treatment The Solution

Key insight: Move the "aha moment" (Process vs Capacity) to slide 7, not slide 16. Executives decide in the first 5 minutes.

Required - Always include
Conditional - Include if applicable
Optional - Can skip for time

OPENING: Hook & Credibility

2-3 minutes
Slide 1 Required
Title + Promise
Client name, data summary, compelling tagline. Sets the stage.
"What are we looking at today?"
Slide 2 Required
The Question
"What happens when patients can't reach you?" Creates curiosity.
"Why should I care about this?"
Slide 3 Required
Who We Are
Brief MyBCAT intro + HIPAA compliance. Builds early trust.
"Who is presenting this and can I trust them?"

DIAGNOSIS: The Discovery

5-7 minutes
Slide 4 Required
Section Divider
"What Your Data Reveals" - Transition to data section.
"What did you find?"
Slide 5 Required
Executive Summary
4 key numbers: Total calls, answer rate, missed calls, revenue leak.
"What are the key numbers?"
Slide 6 Required
The Grade
Answer rate with A-F grading scale. Simple benchmark.
"How do we compare to others?"
Slide 7 Required
Root Cause Discovery
THE AHA MOMENT: Process vs Capacity pie chart. Shows expertise.
"Is this a staffing problem or a process problem?"
Slide 8 Required
Pain Windows
Heatmap + 3 worst hours. Visual proof of when problems occur.
"When are we missing calls?"
Slide 9 Required
Weekly Impact
"X people/week don't get through" - Makes it human.
"How many patients are affected?"

PROGNOSIS: The Cost

3-4 minutes
Slide 10 Required
Section Divider
"What This Costs You" - Transition to impact section.
"So what does this mean for my business?"
Slide 11 Required
Revenue Impact
Conservative $ calculation with clear assumptions.
"How much money are we losing?"
Slide 12 Required
Cost of Inaction
Compound effects: reputation, referrals, staff burden.
"What happens if we do nothing?"
Slide 13 Required
Three Paths Comparison
Do nothing vs Hire staff vs Fix with MyBCAT. Clear choice architecture.
"What are my options?"

TREATMENT: The Solution

5-6 minutes
Slide 14 Required
Section Divider
"How to Fix It" - Transition to solution section.
"What do you recommend?"
Slide 15 Required
What We Provide
Services overview + differentiation from alternatives.
"What exactly do you do?"
Slide 16 Required
Proof Points
Results metrics + test numbers. Social proof.
"Does this actually work?"
Slide 17 Required
FTE-Based Pricing
Cost comparison table: MyBCAT vs In-House. Clear ROI.
"How much does this cost?"
Slide 18 Required
Your Options
Two clear service tiers. Simplifies decision.
"Which option is right for us?"
Slide 19 Required
Next Steps
Clear CTA: "Which option? When to start?"
"What do I do now?"

Presentation Tips

  • For skeptical doctors: Lead with HIPAA compliance and data methodology. They need to trust you before they'll trust your numbers.
  • For busy executives: Jump to slide 7 (Root Cause) quickly. The process vs capacity insight demonstrates expertise.
  • For multi-location practices: Include slide 26 (Location Comparison) - they want to see which site needs the most help.
  • When time is short: Skip Extended Data (slides 20-28) entirely. Core deck (1-19) tells the complete story.
  • For leave-behinds: Include everything. They'll review details later when considering the proposal.
  • Never skip: Slide 7 (Root Cause), Slide 17 (FTE Pricing), Slide 19 (Next Steps) - these drive the sale.
